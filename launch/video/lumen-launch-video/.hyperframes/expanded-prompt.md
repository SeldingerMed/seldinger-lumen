Style block: Use #090B10 background, #111720 panels, #344154 structural strokes, #F2F6FA text, #AAB6C4 muted text, #68D7E1 accent, #70D38D safety, and #ECB958 warning. Typography is Inter for prose and JetBrains Mono for telemetry. The product mood is clinical/research, not neon sci-fi.

Rhythm: hook-proof-BUILD-proof-CTA, 18 seconds.

Global rules: Real Lumen captures are the hero in every scene. Text lives in a separate pane or top/bottom telemetry bar. Transitions are blur-through overlays where incoming content covers outgoing content; no exit animations before transition. Background uses subtle grid/rules and muted telemetry, not decorative blobs.

Scene 1, Hook: Viewer sees a real tortuous vascular tree rollout threading a branch safely. The text claim is concise: "Wall-safe endovascular RL, in the open." Media SLIDES in from the right; telemetry DRAWS in as thin rules.

Scene 3, Physics: Viewer sees solver-derived traces for flow diversion and stent-retriever outcomes. Counters COUNT UP; traces HOLD. The copy emphasizes flow/clot/aneurysm coupling.

Scene 4, Close: Viewer sees the real navigation mosaic as proof, then the public repo and page CTA. The close HOLDS long enough to read.

Negative prompt: no synthetic catheter illustration, no neon vessels behind text, no old white-background screenshots, no apologies, no "toy demo" language.
